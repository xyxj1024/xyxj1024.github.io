Raspberry Pi 3 Model B+: Broadcom BCM2837B0, Cortex-A53 (ARMv8) 64-bit quad-core System-on-Chip (MPSoC) @ 1.4GHz

```bash
$ /sys/devices/system/cpu/cpu0/cache
$ lscpu
```

$32$ KiB 2-way set-associative **L1 instruction cache** and $32$ KiB 4-way set-associative **L1 data cache** ($128$ cache sets) per core, and a $512$ KiB 16-way set-associative **shared L2 cache** ($512$ cache sets)[^1].

Pseudo-random cache replacement policy (makes the Prime+Probe attack much more difficult)

The Cortex-A53 TFR does not state the inclusiveness of the L2 cache.

<!-- excerpt-end -->

Cache-timing side channel attacks and "implicit cache lockdown" of ARM Cortex processors.
